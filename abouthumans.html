Affects on Humans Post Spill

  • Illness: Parents that had income loss due to the spill were 1.5 times more likely to report new physical or mental health problems in their children

  • Physical impacts: Chest pain, coughing, dizziness, headaches, troubles with breathing and vomiting as a result from exposure to hydrocarbons or hydrogen sulfide

  • Death: 11 crew works died in the explosion

  • Poverty: Disaster made people impoverished because these people depended on this water for food, culture, environmental enrichment and recreation.
